Explore Nepal's Hidden Treasures

Discover unique stories and authentic experiences from rural communities across Nepal

Humla

Hello, I'm Dorje from Humla. During autumn, we harvest yarsagumba, a rare medicinal herb found only in the high Himalayas above 4000 meters.

Mugu

Hi, I'm Lakpa from Mugu. Our village surrounds the sacred Rara Lake. We trade salt with Tibet and practice ancient Bon traditions.

Bajura

Namaste! I'm Kalpana from Bajura. We cultivate apples in our mountain orchards and weave traditional Dhaka fabric that takes weeks to complete by hand.

Bardiya

Namaste! I'm Prakash from Bardiya. Our Tharu community lives in traditional mud houses with beautiful wall paintings. We share our land with wild elephants.

Dolpa

Hello, I'm Karma from Dolpa. Shey Phoksundo Lake, our sacred turquoise jewel, is Nepal's deepest lake. We still practice Tibetan Buddhism and trade with yak caravans.

Jajarkot

Hi, I'm Buddhi from Jajarkot. Our women create beautiful Namda felt carpets from local sheep wool during winter months. Each carpet tells a story.

Rolpa

Namaste! I'm Sunita from Rolpa. We grow organic cardamom and mandarin oranges. Our village celebrates Maghe Sankranti with traditional Madal drum performances.

Dang

Hello! I'm Goma from Dang. Our Dang valley is known for organic vegetables and traditional Tharu Maghauta stick dance performed during harvest time.

Mustang

Namaste! I'm Tsering from Mustang. Our ancient caves and monasteries tell stories of the old Tibetan kingdom. We celebrate Tiji festival with masked dances.

Manang

Hi, I'm Dawa from Manang. We celebrate Yartung festival every year with horse racing and archery. Our village sits at 3,500m with views of Annapurna.

Lamjung

Hello! I'm Bishnu from Lamjung. Our village produces the finest honey and we practice traditional Gurung culture with Rodhi gatherings for youth.

Rasuwa

Hello! I'm Pemba from Rasuwa. We make yak cheese and butter in the Langtang valley. Our village was rebuilt with love after the 2015 earthquake.

Sindhupalchok

Namaste! I'm Maya from Sindhupalchok. We grow sweet oranges on terraced hills and celebrate Sonam Losar with our Tamang community.

Gorkha

Namaste! I'm Ram from Gorkha, birthplace of Prithvi Narayan Shah. We maintain ancient Newari architecture and cultivate terraced rice fields using traditional methods.

Kavre

Hi, I'm Sarita from Kavre. We grow organic coffee on our hillsides and celebrate Yomari Punhi by making sweet rice dumplings in sacred shapes.

Chitwan

Hello! I'm Sita from Chitwan. Our national park is home to one-horned rhinos and Bengal tigers. We practice Tharu culture with stick dances and colorful festivals.

Parsa

Namaste! I'm Urmila from Parsa. We grow sugarcane and make traditional sweets. Our village celebrates Chhath festival by the river with devotion.

Dolakha

Hi, I'm Kamala from Dolakha. Here, our women weave beautiful woolen blankets and shawls during winter. We also harvest wild honey from cliff hives.

Ramechhap

Hi, I'm Sanjay from Ramechhap. Our village produces handmade Lokta paper from Daphne plant bark. This paper lasts over 1,000 years!

Sindhuli

Namaste! I'm Pramila from Sindhuli. We cultivate ginger and turmeric in our terraced farms. Our BP Highway connects us while we preserve mountain traditions.

Okhaldhunga

Hi, I'm Indra from Okhaldhunga. We practice organic farming on steep slopes and maintain ancient Rai traditions. Our village produces excellent millet wine.

Khotang

Namaste! I'm Sangita from Khotang. We grow cardamom under forest shade and our villages celebrate Ubhauli and Udhauli migration festivals with traditional dances.

Udayapur

Namaste! I'm Kabita from Udayapur. Our Limbu and Rai communities grow rice and vegetables. We celebrate harvest with traditional Dhan Nach dance.

Solukhumbu

Hello! I'm Pasang from Solukhumbu. We are Sherpa people living in the shadow of Mount Everest. We guide mountaineers and brew traditional chang.

Sankhuwasabha

Hello! I'm Nim from Sankhuwasabha. We collect Chiraito, a precious medicinal herb, and our Rai community performs Sakela dance during planting season.

Taplejung

Namaste! I'm Dhan from Taplejung. We cultivate Himalayan tea under the shadow of Kanchenjunga. Our Limbu community celebrates Chasok Tangnam harvest festival.

Terhathum

Hi, I'm Ganesh from Terhathum. We grow cardamom, ginger and oranges. Our village celebrates both Limbu Chasok and Nepali festivals with equal joy.

Bhojpur

Hello! I'm Tanka from Bhojpur. We grow famous Bhojpuri tea and our blacksmiths make traditional Khukuri knives using ancient techniques.

Dhankuta

Namaste! I'm Shova from Dhankuta. Our town is famous for traditional sweets called Sikarni and handwoven textiles. We maintain rich Newar culture in the hills.

Panchthar

Hi, I'm Laxmi from Panchthar. Our hills produce aromatic tea and cardamom. We celebrate Limbu New Year with traditional Chyabrung drum dances.

Ilam

Hello! I'm Bikash from Ilam. Our misty hills produce Nepal's finest orthodox tea. We handpick tea leaves at sunrise when they're covered in dew.

Siraha

Namaste! I'm Mina from Siraha. Our Maithili community practices traditional fish farming and creates beautiful Mithila paintings that tell ancient stories.

Saptari

Hi, I'm Rupa from Saptari. We cultivate rice, fish, and practice Mithila art. Our women create intricate paintings on walls during festivals.

Sunsari

Hello! I'm Rajesh from Sunsari. We grow jute and rice in fertile plains. Our Koshi River brings blessings and our communities celebrate Chhath with devotion.

Morang

Namaste! I'm Sunita from Morang. We cultivate tea, jute and lentils. Our diverse community celebrates both Kirat and Madhesi festivals throughout the year.

Jhapa

Hi, I'm Ramesh from Jhapa. Our plains produce rice, tea, and vegetables. We blend Nepali, Indian and indigenous cultures in beautiful harmony.

About PahiloPaila

"PahiloPaila" means "First Step" in Nepali. We are that first step towards discovering the real Nepal - beyond Kathmandu, beyond Everest Base Camp.

Founded by a team of Nepali travel enthusiasts and technologists, we believe that the most memorable travel experiences happen when you step off the tourist trail. Our platform bridges the gap between curious travelers and passionate local guides in rural Nepal.

From the high Himalayas of Mustang to the Terai plains of Chitwan, we've partnered with over 150+ local guides across 40+ rural communities. Every journey creates meaningful connections, supports local economies, and preserves traditional cultures.
